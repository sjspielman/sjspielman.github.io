Resources

This page contains (mostly) third-party resources you can use to learn computing for data science and bioinformatics, focusing on open source and reproducible approaches. These resources include online short courses, workshops, blog posts, and books (some $$) that I’ve found over the years which seem useful for others.

Do you have or know of a resource you think I should include? Let me know with an issue here!

Comprehensive resources

  • Introduction to Data Science course (2023) by Rafael Irizarry
    • A full college-level semester course in data science with R, git, and Quarto
  • Materials to learn bioinformatics at home, compiled by Harvard Informatics
    • Links out to various UNIX, R, Python, statistics, git(hub), and miscellaneous omics resources
  • Practical Computing for Biologists, by Steve Haddock and Casey Dunn
    • A beginner-level introduction to UNIX, regular expressions, Python, and general skills in computing
  • Bioinformatics Data Skills, by Vince Buffalo
    • An intermediate-level resource on UNIX skills for bioinformatics, reproducible, project management, R, and “big data” genomics analyses
  • Software Carpentry and Data Carpentry
    • Free lessons and curricula for learning computing and data management skills
